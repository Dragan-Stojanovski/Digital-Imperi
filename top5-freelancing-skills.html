What is a Freelancer?

The freelancer does not work for a company but works for himself(Self-employed). He can make cooperation agreements with companies, but he is still self-employed. The advantage of Freelancing is that you have the freedom to determine your working hours, and how much you will charge, whether per hour or project. Freelancers are not counted as employees of the companies they work with but as collaborators.


Pros and Cons of Freelancing

There are positive and negative things in every job. There is no job that is perfect in all aspects and so is freelancing.


Pros of Freelancing

You have control over where you work, under how much pressure you will work, you have the opportunity to choose which clients you will cooperate with and which you will not, unlike working in a company where you have to work according to what is given to you. And most importantly, you decide for yourself how much money you will work for and all the money remains for you, no company will keep a part for itself and give you a part of the earnings.


Cons of Freelancing

Starting as a freelancer and getting your first projects when nobody knows anything about you can be a pain in the ass. The first projects are the hardest to get after a few positive reviews the whole process is much easier. You're not registered you don't have health insurance, you will have to fix it yourself, you don't have paid sick leave, and you don't even have a secure job, for a small glitch you can be banned from the platform you work on, which will set you back a lot in your career. But still, the freedom that is given from freelancing can't be compared with anything else, and it's worth giving a try.

Self-branding is important and key to boosting your profile and profit.

Many new freelancers have trouble finding their first projects to work on, so at first, you can do a few projects for free for a friend. Those projects will help you get your first real job. From how you will gain the necessary experience and knowledge, one of the best ways to advance your career as a freelancer is to brand yourself with the help of a striking logo and Personal Website (Portfolio).


Personal logo: A personal logo will help you to be impressive and recognizable, you will also look more serious in front of your potential customers. Whether it is a modern logo designed by you or just a slogan with a specific font, use it on your personal website or your social network to let people know it and what it represents.


-Personal website(Portfolio):Your personal website will present you and have the information you want your customers to know about you such as contact information and see more about your work and your completed projects that you have worked on. You will also let them know that you are not hiding and that you are proud of what you do.

3. Website Design

Nowadays, having a website is not a luxury but a necessity that you must not miss. Google searches 1.2 trillion times if you do not have a website, that is 1.2 trillion missed chances. So every business that gets its own modern, useful website gets close to all of this potential customers searching on google.
What is Web Design? Website design is the process of gathering information, market research, creative design, and innovative ideas that are enjoyable for site visitors. You need to give your customers a unique design that will be visually pleasing to them and user-friendly. Webdesigner means that you are going to design a website not develop it but you still need basic knowledge of basic programming languages (HTML, CSS, JavaScript Python, PHP, MySql). 4. Website Development

Website development comes after the design is completed, it is desirable that you have a basic knowledge of design tools to know how to properly develop the design you have received from the design team. The main responsibilities of a website developer are to translate the design into code by keeping every detail as if the design has not been spared and at the same time developing it so that it will be suitable for using devices with different screen sizes. A minimum basic knowledge of programming languages (HTML, CSS, JavaScript Python, PHP, MySql) ​​is required to be able to develop a website.
